## Main User/Operator Flows

- A visitor lands on a real `/` marketing surface with the Allbert brand,
  SEO/OG metadata, and variant-registry CTAs, then enters the app shell.
- An operator works in a shell carrying the Allbert logo/wordmark instead of
  the stock Phoenix asset.
- An operator sees entrance/drawer/skeleton motion that collapses to instant
  when their reduced-motion preference is set.
- An operator reads `/workspace`, `/jobs`, `/objectives`, and the operator
  panels with real visual hierarchy and populated empty/first-run states.
- A first-run operator on an empty workspace sees "what can I do"
  suggested-action affordances (view-only; seats the v0.61 wizard).
- An operator with `system` theme selected gets a dark theme resolved from the
  OS `prefers-color-scheme` preference.

## Data Flow

Brand assets, motion, hierarchy, dark-mode resolution, and SEO/OG metadata are
presentation concerns rendered through the unified catalog inside one app shell.
The landing surface emits the shell data contract, consumes tokens, and uses the
variant registry. Motion durations/easings come from the v0.58 motion-scale
tokens; the reduced-motion axis gates them. Dark mode resolves app tokens from
the operator's theme setting plus OS `prefers-color-scheme`. Suggested-action
affordances and landing CTAs compose registered-action DTOs (ADR 0073) for
display only; no backend, model, or data-layer path is added.

## Authority Invariants

No new capability and no authority change. The catalog stays the rendering
boundary (ADR 0030/0074). Brand, motion, hierarchy, landing, dark mode, and
suggested-action affordances are presentation-only. Suggested actions and
landing CTAs render registered-action DTOs and grant no authority or egress;
launcher selection stays view-only. No model- or data-generated markup. No new
route beyond the landing surface. No internal rename. SEO/OG metadata is static
and exposes no operator data or secrets.
