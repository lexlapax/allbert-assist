# Allbert v0.60 Web UX Professional Pass

Request flow: `docs/plans/v0.60-request-flow.md`
ADR: `docs/adr/0074-web-design-system-and-ux-language.md` (v0.60 amendment:
brand identity, motion layer, marketing surface, and the professional craft
pass; extends ADR 0030's catalog rendering boundary).

Status: planned (pre-1.0 product release 1 of 3).

Source: `docs/plans/roadmap.md` `## v0.60: Web UX Professional Pass`; the v0.58
maturity review (2026-06-25) that found a strong token/accessibility substrate
under a flat operator-utility surface.

v0.60 is the first of three pre-1.0 product releases (v0.60 web UX, v0.61
onboarding & profiles, v0.62 the next product surface). It turns the v0.58
design-system substrate — tokens, variant registry, pattern library, one app
shell, and the unified catalog — into a polished product surface for the
technical-prosumer 1.0 audience, without changing authority or the catalog
rendering boundary.

## Purpose

Close the gap the v0.58 review found between substrate and surface: zero
animation, an absent brand identity (the shipped logo is the stock Phoenix
asset and is not used in-product), a thin landing page with no marketing
surface, a flat visual hierarchy, and a `system` dark-mode setting that falls
back to light. v0.60 delivers brand, motion, hierarchy, a real landing surface,
suggested-action affordances, and OS-driven dark mode — all on the existing
token scales, all through the catalog.

## Expected Inputs

- v0.58 implemented and accepted: token set (color, type, spacing, radius,
  motion scales), the prop-driven component-variant registry, the pattern
  library, one app shell, and the catalog as the rendering boundary for every
  page.
- ADR 0074 accepted, with the v0.60 amendment as the scope contract.
- The reduced-motion and high-contrast accessibility axes already in the token
  system.
- The ADR-accepted thin-landing shape at `/` (to be replaced by a real landing
  surface).
- Current `/workspace`, `/jobs`, `/objectives`, and the v0.58 operator panels
  (Intents, Settings/Models, Surface Policy).

## Operator-Visible Win

An operator lands on a real `/` marketing surface with the Allbert brand,
navigates into a shell carrying the wordmark instead of the stock framework
logo, sees entrance/drawer/skeleton motion that respects their reduced-motion
preference, reads a workspace with real visual hierarchy and populated
first-run/empty states that suggest what to do next, and gets a dark theme that
honors their OS preference when `system` is selected — with no change to what
the app can do on their behalf.

## Planned Interfaces

- Brand asset set (logo/wordmark, favicon/app icons, OG image) applied across
  the shell and landing surface, retiring the stock Phoenix asset.
- A motion layer: entrance, drawer, and skeleton/loading transitions expressed
  through the existing motion-scale tokens, gated by the reduced-motion axis.
- A landing/marketing surface at `/` with SEO/OG metadata, replacing the
  thin-landing ADR exception.
- Suggested-action / "what can I do" affordances on empty/first workspace views.
- OS dark-mode resolution so `system` resolves app tokens to the OS preference.

## Brand Identity And Landing Surface (M1)

A real logo/wordmark replaces the stock Phoenix asset everywhere it appears
(shell header, mobile shellbar, favicon, app icons). The brand is expressed as
catalog/token-consuming assets and CSS, not as model- or data-generated markup.

`/` becomes a real landing/marketing surface — still rendered through the
catalog inside (or as an accepted shape that emits) the shell data contract,
consuming tokens and the variant registry — with SEO and Open Graph metadata
(title, description, canonical, OG/Twitter card, OG image). This replaces the
ADR-accepted thin-landing exception noted in ADR 0074 consequences. No new
route beyond this landing surface; `/workspace` stays canonical.

## Motion Layer (M2)

An entrance/drawer/skeleton motion layer over the existing v0.58 motion-scale
tokens. No new animation primitives outside the token scale; durations and
easings are token-driven. Every transition is gated by the reduced-motion axis
already in the token system: with reduced-motion set, transitions collapse to
instant. Motion is presentation-only and introduces no new state or authority.

## Visual-Hierarchy Craft Pass (M3)

A depth/emphasis/density pass across `/workspace`, `/jobs`, `/objectives`, and
the operator panels, plus populated empty/first-run states. Executed by
extending the variant registry and pattern library — elevation/surface tones,
emphasis variants, density options, and empty-state atoms — not by hand-rolling
per-page HEEx. Jobs and Objectives stay folded into the catalog/shell (no
regression of the v0.58 consolidation). This is craft on the existing
component contract, not a layout rebuild.

## Dark Mode And Suggested-Action Affordances (M4)

OS dark-mode resolution: the `system` setting resolves app tokens to the OS
`prefers-color-scheme` preference instead of falling back to light, across all
pages, preserving the explicit light/dark overrides. Suggested-action / "what
can I do" affordances appear on empty/first workspace views as view-only
entry points that compose registered-action DTOs through the catalog — they
grant no authority and seat the v0.61 onboarding wizard without implementing it
here.

## Security Posture

No new authority and no authority change. The catalog stays the rendering
boundary (ADR 0030/0074); brand, motion, hierarchy, landing, and
suggested-action affordances are all presentation-only. Suggested actions and
landing CTAs render registered-action DTOs (ADR 0073) and grant no authority or
egress; launcher selection stays view-only. No model- or data-generated markup
is introduced. No internal rename (the v0.58 "Conversations" UI-string
convention is unchanged). No new route beyond the landing surface. SEO/OG
metadata is static and exposes no operator data or secrets.

## Non-Goals

- No model-generated UI; the catalog remains the rendering boundary.
- No new routes beyond the landing surface.
- No new authority, capability, or egress; no authority change.
- No internal rename; "Conversations" stays UI-string-only.
- No substrate rebuild (canvas, tiles, ephemerals, Fragment/HMAC, persistence
  are untouched).
- No onboarding wizard implementation (seated here, built in v0.61).
- No new animation primitives outside the v0.58 motion-scale tokens.
- No backend, model, or data-layer changes.
