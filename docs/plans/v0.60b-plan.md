(0.60.0, Product Experience Design) and v0.61 (Presentation Layer Overhaul).

## Purpose

v0.60 designed the product's **structure**: the unified technical-prosumer
journey, the information architecture, the navigation model, the screen
composition, and a navigable walking skeleton (ADR 0077). v0.58 shipped a
**component-contract baseline, not a final UX layout redesign** (ADR 0074 §57).
Between them, one layer was left **UNOWNED**: the *ultra-modern visual / UX / UI
design language* — the art direction, the aesthetic, the visual-hierarchy and
motion character, the "does this feel like a 1.0 product" layer.

The v0.60 walking skeleton made this gap concrete and visible. Rendered through
the current operator-utility-flat shell, the product **shape** is structurally
sound — the IA is navigable, the composition zones are right — but it reads as
**visually utilitarian, not modern**. The skeleton proves the structure; it does
not answer "what should this product *look and feel* like at 1.0." That is a
design decision, and — exactly as ADR 0077 argued for structure — it must be made
**design-first, before v0.61 builds**, not discovered by v0.61 while it is
implementing screens. A visual language discovered during the v0.61 overhaul (or
worse, during the v0.64 RC) would churn every redesigned screen and risk landing
an incoherent aesthetic on the presentation contracts v1.0 freezes.

v0.60b is that fix. It is the **parallel visual-direction release** to what ADR
0077 did for structural direction: a small, design-first point release that
produces the visual language v0.61 then implements. Concretely it:

1. **Studies the ultra-modern bar** — modern AI / desktop / developer tools,
   local-first AI apps, and current design systems — and extracts the visual and
   interaction principles relevant to a **trust-first local assistant** (M1).
2. **Writes the visual-language brief and the evaluation rubric** — the
   requirements the language MUST satisfy (the v0.60 IA / journey / trust surfaces,
   the technical-prosumer persona, token/catalog extensibility, the a11y axes, the
   chat-primary composition, local-first performance) and the scoring rubric the
   operator will use to choose (M2).
3. **Produces at least THREE divergent candidate design directions** — each a
   coherent, distinct visual/UX language (type, color, spacing/density, motion
   character, layout/composition philosophy, the chat-primary hero composition),
   each **VIEWABLE as rendered hero screens** (workspace, onboarding, trust,
   landing) so the operator evaluates real pixels, not prose (M3).
4. **Evaluates the directions comparatively** against the M2 rubric as a
   decision-ready side-by-side (M4).
5. **Records the operator's choice** of ONE canonical visual language as ADR 0079,
   with the design-system token/component delta v0.61 must build and the v0.61
   build handoff (M5).
6. **Proves the chosen language** by applying it to the walking-skeleton hero
   screens as a hi-fi rendered proof — real, not only mocks (M6).
7. **Gates the release** with `:v060b` evals and `mix allbert.test release.v060b`
   (M7).

The core deliverable is simple: **≥3 viewable design directions → the operator
chooses one → the canonical visual language is handed to v0.61.** The authority
boundary is unchanged. Any code v0.60b produces (styled hero-screen renderings) is
disposable design-exploration behind the preview flag; the catalog stays the
rendering boundary; Security Central grants nothing new.

## Foundations & Handoff
- **Builds on:**
  - **v0.60 Product Experience Design (ADR 0077)** — the IA, journey, persona, and
    trust structure the visual language dresses, and the navigable walking skeleton
    whose hero screens (workspace, onboarding, trust, landing) v0.60b re-skins into
    viewable candidate renderings. v0.60b designs the *look* of the structure v0.60
    designed; it does not re-open the IA.
  - **The v0.58 design system (ADR 0030 / 0073 / 0074)** — the design tokens (color,
    type, spacing, radius, motion scales), the prop-driven component-variant
    registry, the pattern library, one app shell, and the unified catalog as the
    rendering boundary. The candidate directions are expressed as **token / variant
    deltas over this substrate**, not a new rendering mechanism; the selected
    direction's delta is what v0.61 grows.
  - **ADR 0077** — the structural-direction precedent v0.60b mirrors for visual
    direction (design-first, before the v0.61 build).
- **Hands off to:**
  - **v0.61 Presentation Layer Overhaul** — consumes the **chosen** canonical
    visual language (ADR 0079 + `docs/design/visual-language-selected.md`) and the
    design-system token/component **delta**, and IMPLEMENTS that visual language
    over the v0.60 IA as the polished primary 1.0 surface, landing **before the
    v1.0 presentation-contract freeze**. v0.61 previously consumed only the v0.60
    IA/skeleton; with v0.60b inserted, v0.61's brand/motion/visual-hierarchy pass
    now implements a **chosen** language rather than inventing one.
  - **Downstream v0.62 (packaging), v0.63 (onboarding), v0.64 (product RC), and
    v1.0 (freeze) are unchanged.** v0.60b adds a design decision v0.61 consumes; it
    does not touch the packaging, onboarding, or RC scope, and it does not move any
    freeze.

## Cross-Cutting Invariants (apply to every milestone)
- **Design-first; no new runtime behavior.** v0.60b adds no capability class, no
  authority surface, no confirmation-floor change, no egress grant, and **no
  Settings key** (see the keys table). Security Central is unchanged and remains
  the sole authority boundary.
- **Any code is disposable design-exploration, not the v0.61 build.** The styled
  hero-screen renderings (M3 candidates, M6 proof) exist only to make the
  directions **viewable as real pixels**. They live behind the existing
  `:preview_routes` compile-env flag, render placeholder-only compositions through
  the catalog, read no live data, and grant no authority. v0.61 does not inherit
  this exploration code as its build; it inherits the **chosen language spec** and
  re-implements it properly.
- **The catalog stays the rendering boundary.** Every candidate rendering composes
  through the unified catalog inside the one app shell (ADR 0030/0073/0074). The
  candidate directions are token/variant/theme **overrides** applied through the
  catalog; no model-generated or data-generated markup is introduced.
- **≥3 divergent directions, one operator choice.** v0.60b is not "pick a theme": it
  produces at least three *coherent and distinct* visual languages, evaluates them
  against a written rubric, and the **operator chooses exactly one** as canonical.
  The choice is recorded in ADR 0079 and `visual-language-selected.md`.
- **No IA re-open, no structural change.** v0.60b dresses the v0.60 IA; it does not
  add, remove, or re-order screens, routes, or the navigation model. If a direction
  implies a structural change, that is out of scope and folds back to ADR 0077, not
  here.
- **One spine.** Nothing in v0.60b reaches `Settings.Store`, `Confirmations`, or
  business logic directly; the renderings consume the catalog/shell/token contract
  only.

### M1: Reference & Competitive Research
**Status:** planned.

Study the "ultra-modern" bar and extract the visual and interaction principles
relevant to a trust-first local assistant. Survey the reference set — modern AI
chat / assistant products, modern desktop and developer tools, local-first AI apps,
and current design systems — and distil, for each, *what makes it read as modern*:
type systems, color and surface treatment, spacing/density, motion character,
elevation/depth, layout and composition philosophy, and the interaction feel. The
output is a **mood / direction inventory**: named visual directions the reference
set clusters into (not a single recommendation yet), each tied to concrete
references and to what it would mean for a **local-first, trust-first** assistant
whose primary surface is chat.

The research is explicitly framed for Allbert's constraints: it flags which modern
patterns fit a trust-first local product (calm, legible, honest affordances) and
which do not (dark-pattern urgency, cloud-service chrome, novelty motion that
undercuts trust). It is descriptive of the *bar*, not prescriptive of the choice —
M2 turns it into requirements and M3 turns it into directions.

- **Implementation:**
  - **Artifacts:** `docs/design/visual-language-research.md` — the reference survey,
    the per-reference "what makes it modern" extraction, the visual/interaction
    principles, and the mood/direction inventory (named clusters) with a trust-first
    fit note per cluster.
  - **Inputs:** the ultra-modern reference set (modern AI/desktop/dev tools,
    local-first AI apps, current design systems); the v0.60 product-experience-spec
    (the technical-prosumer audience and the trust position); the v0.58 token
    substrate (what the tokens can and cannot currently express).
  - **Outputs:** the principles and mood/direction inventory M2 turns into the brief
    + rubric and M3 turns into ≥3 concrete directions.
  - **Verify:** the M7 `visual-language-research-present-001` eval row (the
    `v060b_sweep_eval_test.exs` row that `File.read!`s
    `docs/design/visual-language-research.md` and asserts the reference survey, the
    extracted principles, and the mood/direction inventory) plus request-flow **S1**.
- **Acceptance:** the research doc surveys the ultra-modern reference set, extracts
  the visual/interaction principles, presents a named mood/direction inventory with
  a trust-first fit note per direction, and is docs-gate clean.

### M2: Visual-Language Brief & Evaluation Rubric
**Status:** planned.

Write the **visual-language brief** — the requirements the language MUST satisfy —
and the **evaluation rubric** the operator will use to choose among the three
directions. The brief pins the constraints the aesthetic is drawn against:

- **The v0.60 surfaces it must dress** — the IA / journey / trust surfaces and the
  screen-composition rules from ADR 0077 (the visual language decorates this
  structure, it does not change it).
- **The technical-prosumer persona** — the audience the "feel" targets (credible,
  capable, calm; not consumer-toy, not enterprise-grey).
- **Catalog / token extensibility** — every direction must be expressible as a
  delta over the v0.58 token scales and component-variant registry, through the
  catalog; nothing that requires abandoning the rendering boundary.
- **The a11y axes** — dark, high-contrast, and reduced-motion must each remain
  first-class; a direction that only works in one mode is disqualified.
- **The chat-primary composition** — chat is the primary surface (ADR 0074 §71 /
  ADR 0077); the hero composition centers on it.
- **Performance / local-first** — the aesthetic must render fast on prosumer
  hardware and not depend on heavy assets or network calls.

The **rubric** turns these into scored axes (fit to IA/journey/persona/trust; "does
it feel 1.0 / ultra-modern"; implementability on the token/catalog system; a11y
across the three modes; performance/local-first) with a scoring scale and weighting,
so M4's comparison and M5's operator choice are made against one explicit yardstick
rather than taste alone.

- **Implementation:**
  - **Artifacts:** `docs/design/visual-language-brief.md` — the must-satisfy
    requirements (IA/journey/trust surfaces, technical-prosumer persona,
    catalog/token extensibility, the dark/high-contrast/reduced-motion a11y axes,
    chat-primary composition, performance/local-first) **and** the scoring rubric
    (axes, scale, weighting) in an explicit `## Evaluation Rubric` section.
  - **Inputs:** the M1 principles + mood/direction inventory; the v0.60 IA
    (`information-architecture.md`), journey (`product-experience-spec.md`), and
    persona (`persona-model.md`) surfaces; the v0.58 token/catalog contract; the
    M7 (v0.60) `design-system-gap-analysis.md` (what the token system already lacks).
  - **Outputs:** the requirements M3 designs each direction to satisfy; the rubric
    M4 scores against and M5 chooses with.
  - **Verify:** the M7 `visual-language-brief-present-001` and
    `visual-language-rubric-present-001` eval rows (the `v060b_sweep_eval_test.exs`
    rows that `File.read!` `docs/design/visual-language-brief.md`, assert the
    requirement set and the `## Evaluation Rubric` section with scored axes) plus
    request-flow **S1**.
- **Acceptance:** the brief enumerates the must-satisfy requirements (IA/journey/
  trust, persona, token/catalog extensibility, the three a11y axes, chat-primary
  composition, performance/local-first); the rubric defines scored axes with a scale
  and weighting; both are docs-gate clean.

### M3: Three Divergent Design Directions
**Status:** planned.

Produce **at least three** coherent, distinct visual/UX languages, each satisfying
the M2 brief and each **VIEWABLE as rendered hero screens**. Each direction is a
complete visual language — not a color swap — specifying:

- **Type** — the type system (families, scale, weight, rhythm).
- **Color** — palette, surface/elevation tones, semantic colors, dark/high-contrast
  behavior.
- **Spacing / density** — the spacing scale and the density posture (airy vs
  compact) for a chat-primary prosumer surface.
- **Motion character** — the motion personality (calm/precise vs lively), always
  with a reduced-motion collapse.
- **Layout / composition philosophy** — how zones, panels, and the primary chat
  surface are composed; the elevation/depth model.
- **The chat-primary hero composition** — the signature composition of the primary
  workspace with chat as the hero.

The directions must be **divergent**: they explore genuinely different points in
the design space (e.g. a warm editorial calm, a precise technical/monospace-forward
console, a soft modern glassy depth — illustrative, not prescriptive), so the
operator's choice is a real fork, not three shades of one idea.

Each direction is rendered as the **four hero screens** — **workspace, onboarding,
trust, landing** — so the operator evaluates real pixels. **Recommended
mechanism:** styled variants of the v0.60 walking-skeleton hero screens, rendered
and screenshotted exactly like the v0.60 S4.5 browser pass. Static hi-fi mocks are
acceptable **only if** produced with a design tool and captured as images. The
renderings apply each direction's token/variant/theme delta **through the catalog**,
behind the existing `:preview_routes` flag, with no live data and no authority.

- **Implementation:**
  - **Artifacts:** `docs/design/visual-direction-a.md`,
    `docs/design/visual-direction-b.md`, `docs/design/visual-direction-c.md`
    (**≥3**; each specifying type / color / spacing-density / motion /
    layout-composition / chat-primary hero composition, plus the token/variant delta
    that expresses it) **and** the rendered hero-screen sets per direction (workspace
    / onboarding / trust / landing).
  - **Modules / Files (if using the recommended rendered-variant mechanism):** a
    flag-gated `/preview/visual/<direction>/<screen>` route set (reusing the existing
    `:preview_routes` compile-env flag) backed by a styled-variant LiveView that
    layers each direction's token/theme delta over the v0.60
    `AllbertAssistWeb.Skeleton.PreviewLive` hero compositions; a small
    `AllbertAssistWeb.Skeleton.VisualDirectionManifest` listing the direction ids and
    their hero-screen paths. No new catalog atoms, no business-state reads, no
    model-/data-generated markup; the fallback unknown-component placeholder must not
    appear in successful evidence. **This route/variant code is disposable
    exploration, not the v0.61 build.**
  - **Inputs:** the M2 brief + rubric (each direction must satisfy the brief); the
    M1 mood/direction inventory; the v0.60 walking-skeleton hero screens and the v0.58
    token/variant substrate.
  - **Settings:** none (see the keys table).
  - **Tests:** if the rendered-variant mechanism is used,
    `apps/allbert_assist_web/test/allbert_assist_web/skeleton/visual_direction_proof_test.exs`
    (tagged `:v060b_visual_direction`) asserts every
    `/preview/visual/<direction>/<screen>` route resolves through the catalog/shell,
    that **≥3** directions each render the four hero screens, that each direction's
    root carries its distinguishing token/theme attributes, that the a11y axes hold,
    and that no screen reads business state or composes an effectful affordance. It
    emits the pinned status line the request-flow **S4**/**S5** grep verbatim:
    `hero-renderings-present-001 status=pass directions=3 hero_screens_per_direction=4 live_data=false authority=none`.
  - **Outputs:** the ≥3 rendered directions M4 scores and the operator views
    side-by-side in the request-flow **S4.5** design-review pass.
  - **Verify:** the M7 design-artifact rows (`visual-direction-a/b/c-present-001`)
    and the `hero-renderings-present-001` row; request-flow **S3** (directions
    present + divergent + each facet specified), **S4** (renderings resolve), and
    the **S4.5** design-review browser pass (operator views the three side-by-side).
- **Acceptance:** at least three coherent, divergent visual languages are specified
  (each covering type / color / spacing-density / motion / layout-composition /
  chat-primary hero composition) and each is viewable as the four rendered hero
  screens (workspace / onboarding / trust / landing); the renderings are
  placeholder-only through the catalog with no live data or authority; docs-gate
  clean.

### M4: Comparative Evaluation
**Status:** planned.

Score the ≥3 directions against the M2 rubric and assemble a **decision-ready

directly (the pixels are the evidence).

  - **Outputs:** the decision-ready comparison M5's operator choice reads.

### M5: Selection & Canonical Visual-Language Spec
**Status:** planned.

The **operator CHOOSES one direction.** Record the choice and its rationale as the
canonical visual language: move **ADR 0079 to Accepted-with-choice (v0.60b)** with
the chosen direction named and justified against the rubric, and write the canonical
spec. The spec pins the chosen language definitively (type / color / spacing-density
/ motion / layout-composition / chat-primary hero composition) and — critically —
specifies the **design-system token/component gap DELTA v0.61 must build**: the new
or changed tokens (surface/elevation tones, density scale, motion presets), the new
or extended component variants, and the new patterns the chosen language needs
beyond the v0.58 substrate. It closes with the **v0.61 build handoff**: what v0.61
implements, over the v0.60 IA, to realize this language.

The operator's choice is recorded in the request-flow **S4.5** design-review pass
(the operator views the three renderings side-by-side and records the chosen
direction), and that recorded decision is what M5 formalizes here.

- **Implementation:**
  - **Artifacts:** `docs/design/visual-language-selected.md` — the chosen direction,
    the rationale against the rubric, the canonical visual-language spec, the
    **token/component delta** v0.61 builds, and the v0.61 build handoff;
    `docs/adr/0079-visual-design-language-and-art-direction.md` moved to
    **Accepted-with-choice (v0.60b)** naming the selected direction.
