# v0.13 — Local personalization (LoRA / adapter training)

Status: Shipped

## Summary

The origin note asked for a nightly pipeline that distils or retrains a small local model so Allbert's memory and personality tune to the user over time. v0.11 took the first reviewable step: a deterministic `personality-digest` that compiles a markdown learned overlay (`PERSONALITY.md`) from approved durable memory, approved facts, and bounded recent episode summaries, plus a `LearningJob` trait seam. v0.12 established the review-first self-change envelope (sibling worktrees, `patch-approval`, no auto-swap). v0.12.1 made those review surfaces operator-legible; v0.12.2 made them replayable.

v0.13 is the release that actually trains. A `PersonalityAdapterJob` plugs into the v0.11 `LearningJob` seam and trains a small LoRA/adapter from the same approved-durable + approved-fact + bounded-episode-summary corpus the digest already honors, plus `SOUL.md` baseline persona/constraints, accepted `PERSONALITY.md` adaptation hints, and optional v0.12.2 redacted trace excerpts. Every new adapter routes through a new `adapter-approval` inbox kind that mirrors v0.12's `patch-approval`. Training requires a compatible local base model and local trainer backend; activation is local-only (Ollama today; future local providers are additive), single-slot, base-model-pinned, and explicit. Hosted providers ignore the active adapter rather than pretending to apply one.

The release introduces a daily compute-time cap distinct from the ADR 0051 spend cap, an opt-in trainer-backend allowlist scoped to "this binary is permitted as a trainer" without weakening the universal exec policy, and a small additive protocol bump to `5` for adapter management messages and live training progress events. Adapter weights are derived/host-specific and excluded from profile export by default; the corpus inputs continue to travel so a peer machine can re-train its own equivalent adapter against the same digest.

## Goals

- Ship a `PersonalityAdapterJob` that trains a LoRA adapter from the v0.11 corpus contract plus `SOUL.md`, accepted `PERSONALITY.md`, and optional redacted v0.12.2 trace excerpts.
- Add an `AdapterTrainer` trait with three implementations: `MlxLoraTrainer` (Apple Silicon), `LlamaCppLoraTrainer` (cross-platform), and `FakeAdapterTrainer` (deterministic provider-free fake for tests, CI, and explicit dev validation only).
- Persist training runs at `~/.allbert/adapters/runs/<run_id>/`, accepted adapters at `~/.allbert/adapters/installed/<adapter_id>/`, the active-adapter pointer at `~/.allbert/adapters/active.json`, and history at `~/.allbert/adapters/history.jsonl`.
- Add the `adapter-approval` inbox kind with kind-specific frontmatter, eval summary, and accept-vs-activate separation that mirrors `patch-approval`.
- Local-only training and activation. Training requires a compatible local base model plus trainer backend; only the Ollama provider activates an adapter in v0.13. Hosted providers ignore the active-adapter pointer and surface a one-line operator notice.
- Daily compute-time cap (`learning.compute_cap_wall_seconds`) modelled on ADR 0051's spend cap.
- Trainer-backend allowlist (`learning.adapter_training.allowed_backends`) that combines with the existing exec policy as a two-gate opt-in.
- Three fixed evals so non-ML reviewers can decide: behavioral diff, golden regression set, ASCII loss curve.
- Setup wizard step covering the trainer backend, compute cap, and corpus opt-in for v0.12.2 trace excerpts.
- Adapter artifacts excluded from profile export by default; corpus inputs travel as before; opt-in `--include-adapters` for installed-adapter mirrors.
- Operator surfaces in CLI (`allbert-cli adapters …`), TUI (`/adapters …`), and Telegram (`/adapter status`).
- Reuse v0.12.1 boundaries: same activity transitions, same redaction posture, same channel capability gating, same path-preserving settings; do not invent a parallel personalization stack.

## Non-goals

- No training on staged, unapproved, or ephemeral content.
- No automatic activation. Accept installs but does not activate; activation is a separate explicit operator command.
- No multi-adapter stacking. Single-slot in v0.13.
- No hosted-provider training or activation. Hosted providers ignore the adapter; training requires a compatible local base model and local trainer backend, and training data never leaves the machine in v0.13.
- No hosted-training opt-in. The hosted-training seam is documented but not implemented; it would land as a fourth `AdapterTrainer` behind the v0.11 hosted-provider consent gate (ADR 0079).
- No foundation-model retraining or distillation. v0.13 trains adapters on top of a local base, not full models.
- No dependence on the v0.12 Rust rebuild skill. Adapter training does not require recompiling the Allbert binary.
- No new isolation mechanism for code-shaped self-change. Adapters are a sibling artifact kind; sibling worktrees remain v0.12's path.
- No Rust ML framework adoption (`candle`, `burn`, `tch`). Trainers are external executables behind the owned `AdapterTrainer` trait, mirroring ADR 0066.
- No removal of v0.11 personality digest. Adapter training is an additive surface alongside the digest, not a replacement.
- No new approval kinds beyond `adapter-approval`. Memory remediation, patch review, and skill review continue to use existing kinds.

## Plan hardening decisions

These decisions are release-blocking. Implementation should not invent alternative trainer policy, storage shape, activation rules, or migration policy while landing personalization.

### Trainer trait and backends

`AdapterTrainer` is the only surface the kernel calls into for training. v0.13 ships three implementations; future backends plug into the same trait (ADR 0084). `FakeAdapterTrainer` is for provider-free tests, CI, and explicit dev validation; normal setup must not suggest it unless the operator deliberately enables the fake backend. Every real backend invokes external executables only through the existing exec-policy gate plus the kind-scoped `learning.adapter_training.allowed_backends` allowlist (ADR 0089). The kernel constructs all trainer arguments; operators cannot inject free-form arguments.

### Activation envelope

Training and activation are local-only, single-slot, base-model-pinned, and explicit (ADR 0085). A training run requires a compatible local base model and a configured local trainer backend. Ollama is the only v0.13 provider that activates an adapter. Hosted providers silently ignore the active adapter and emit one informational notice per session. Switching the active model auto-deactivates a base-model-mismatched adapter and logs the deactivation; the operator must reactivate explicitly.

### Inbox shape

Adapter approvals use the new `adapter-approval` kind (ADR 0086). Weights live as artifacts referenced by `weights_path`, never inlined. Eval summary, loss curve, and behavioral diff are markdown/JSON artifacts referenced from the approval markdown. Accept installs but does not activate; activation is `adapters activate <id>`. Reject deletes the run directory by default; `learning.adapter_training.keep_rejected_runs` preserves it for forensic review.

### Compute cap

`learning.compute_cap_wall_seconds` is a daily wall-clock ceiling enforced at job dispatch and at progress checkpoints (ADR 0087). `null` or `0` disables the cap; positive values clamp to `[60, 86400]`. The aggregate is read from training-run manifests, keyed by UTC day, cached for 60 seconds. Override is per-run with a recorded reason. Scheduled jobs fail closed at the cap (ADR 0015).

### Continuity

Adapter artifacts are derived/host-specific and excluded from profile export by default (ADR 0088). The corpus inputs (durable memory, accepted facts, bounded episode summaries, accepted `PERSONALITY.md`, `SOUL.md`, opt-in v0.12.2 trace excerpts) continue to travel. `profile export --include-adapters` is the explicit opt-in and includes installed adapters plus `active.json` only; it does not include runs, incoming external adapters, runtime caches, or history. The dry-run output names the exclusions.

### Privacy on training inputs

Secret redaction is unconditional for any training corpus material that originates from v0.12.2 traces (per ADR 0082's contract). v0.13 does not weaken redaction; the redactor is invoked again at corpus-build time as a defensive double-pass. Sessions captured under `capture_messages = false` contribute structural-only summaries; staged-only memory entries are never included.

### ADR readiness

The v0.13 ADRs are accepted. M0 must re-read them before code implementation begins and patch the ADRs first if implementation needs to diverge:

- ADR 0084: PersonalityAdapterJob is a LearningJob with an owned trainer trait.
- ADR 0085: Adapter activation is local-only and base-model-pinned.
- ADR 0086: Adapter approval is a new inbox kind.
- ADR 0087: Adapter compute cap is a wall-clock daily gate.
- ADR 0088: Adapter artifacts are derived/host-specific and excluded from profile export.
- ADR 0089: Trainer subprocess runs under existing exec policy with kind-scoped allowlist.
- ADR 0090: Protocol v5 adapter management and training progress.

## What-if audit findings

| Scenario | Today | v0.13 response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Operator wants the assistant to learn their voice over time | v0.11 ships a deterministic markdown overlay only | `PersonalityAdapterJob` trains a LoRA against the same corpus; review-first activation through `adapter-approval`. |
| Operator runs hosted-only (no local base model/backend) | No compatible local target | Training and activation require a compatible local base model plus trainer backend; hosted provider sessions ignore any active adapter and show the one-line notice. |
| Adapter would memorize a secret | No safeguard if secrets exist in source | v0.12.2 secret redactor runs again at corpus-build time as a defensive double-pass. |
| Operator drops a third-party adapter into `~/.allbert/adapters/` | Undefined | `adapters/incoming/` quarantine routes through `adapter-approval` with `provenance: external`. |
| Training pins the operator's machine for hours | No gate | `learning.compute_cap_wall_seconds` enforced at dispatch and checkpoints; SIGTERM at cap. |
| Base model changes after training | No safeguard | Adapter manifest pins `base_model.{provider, model_id, model_digest}`; activation refuses on `model_id` mismatch and warns on digest mismatch. |
| Operator switches the active model | Adapter remains active and may degrade output | Kernel auto-deactivates mismatched adapter, logs the deactivation, surfaces one-line operator notice. |
| Profile export with adapters present | Undefined | Adapters excluded by default; corpus inputs included; `--include-adapters` explicitly includes installed adapters plus `active.json` only. |
| Training run cancelled mid-flight | Undefined | SIGTERM with 30s grace, then SIGKILL; manifest records `cancelled_at`; cancelled runs cannot be approved. |
| Codex Web / CI machine without GPU runs the test suite | Trainer requires hardware | `FakeAdapterTrainer` produces a deterministic fixture artifact tree; all v0.13 tests run provider-free. |
| Reviewer is not an ML engineer | Loss curves are inscrutable | `adapter-approval` ships eval summary: golden_pass_rate, behavioral diff markdown, ASCII loss curve. |
| Two adapters compete for activation | Undefined | Single-slot pointer; activate is explicit and atomic. |
| Operator wants to roll back | Undefined | `adapters deactivate` returns to base; `adapters activate <prev_id>` switches; `adapters remove <id>` deletes installed weights (refused if active without `--force`). |
| Operator wants the v0.12.2 traces to inform training | Not connected | `learning.adapter_training.capture_traces` opt-in adds redacted trace excerpts to the corpus. |
| Setup on a fresh profile | No personalization step | Setup wizard adds a "Local personalization" step after the trace step. |
| Existing profile upgrades to v0.13 | No `[learning.adapter_training]` block | Daemon attempts path-preserving default insert; if unsafe, loads in-memory defaults and emits a remediation hint. |

## Public interfaces

### Config

```toml
[learning]
enabled = true                              # also gates v0.11 personality-digest
compute_cap_wall_seconds = 7200             # daily cap; null or 0 disables

[learning.adapter_training]
enabled = false                             # opt-in; defaults to disabled
allowed_backends = []                       # populated during setup wizard, e.g. ["mlx-lm-lora", "llama-cpp-finetune", "fake"]
default_backend = ""                        # required when enabled; one of allowed_backends
schedule = ""                               # e.g. "@weekly on saturday at 02:00"; empty disables scheduled training
include_tiers = ["durable", "fact"]
include_episodes = true
episode_lookback_days = 30
max_episode_summaries = 16
max_input_bytes = 262144                    # 256 KiB
max_output_artifact_bytes = 5368709120      # 5 GiB per training run
capture_traces = false                      # opt-in; pulls redacted v0.12.2 traces into corpus
trace_lookback_days = 14
default_lora_rank = 8
max_lora_rank = 64
default_alpha = 16
default_learning_rate = "0.0001"
default_max_steps = 200
default_batch_size = 4
default_seed = 42
min_golden_pass_rate = "0.85"
keep_rejected_runs = false
max_log_bytes = 16777216                    # 16 MiB per stream per run
cancel_grace_seconds = 30
```

`compute_cap_wall_seconds`: `null` or `0` disables enforcement; positive values clamp to `[60, 86400]` (1 minute to 24 hours); out-of-range values warn and clamp at config load.
`max_lora_rank` clamps to `[1, 256]`; `default_lora_rank` must satisfy `<= max_lora_rank`.
`min_golden_pass_rate` clamps to `[0.0, 1.0]`.
`include_tiers` rejects values outside the memory tier enum.
`schedule` follows v0.2's bounded schedule DSL (ADR 0022).
`allowed_backends` rejects unknown values; valid values in v0.13 are `mlx-lm-lora`, `llama-cpp-finetune`, and `fake`.
`default_backend` must be in `allowed_backends` when `learning.adapter_training.enabled = true`; it may be empty while adapter training is disabled.

The v0.12.1 path-preserving TOML edit machinery handles default-write for missing `[learning.adapter_training]` blocks the same way v0.12.2 handles the `[trace]` block. Out-of-range values warn and clamp.

### Daemon protocol

Bump `PROTOCOL_VERSION` from `4` to `5` with additive adapter management messages and live training progress events.

Add:

- `AdapterManifest`: `{ adapter_id, provenance, trainer_backend, base_model, training_run_id, corpus_digest, weights_format, weights_size_bytes, hyperparameters, resource_cost, eval_summary, overall, created_at, accepted_at }`.
- `ActiveAdapter`: `{ adapter_id, base_model, activated_at }`.
- `AdapterTrainingProgressPayload`: `{ run_id, phase, step, total_steps, elapsed_seconds, eta_seconds, peak_resident_mb, last_loss }`.
- `AdapterTrainingFinalPayload`: `{ run_id, status, ended_at, manifest_path, approval_id }`. `status` is one of `succeeded | cancelled | failed | cap-exceeded`.
- `ClientMessage::AdaptersList`, `AdaptersShow`, `AdaptersActivate`, `AdaptersDeactivate`, `AdaptersRemove`, `AdaptersStatus`, `AdaptersHistory`, `AdaptersTrainingStart`, `AdaptersTrainingCancel`, `AdaptersInstallExternal`.
- `ServerMessage::Adapters(Vec<AdapterManifest>)`, `Adapter(AdapterManifest)`, `ActiveAdapter(Option<ActiveAdapter>)`, `AdaptersStatus(AdaptersStatusPayload)`, `AdaptersHistory(Vec<AdaptersHistoryEntry>)`, `AdapterTrainingProgress(AdapterTrainingProgressPayload)`, `AdapterTrainingFinal(AdapterTrainingFinalPayload)`.

Backward compatibility:

- v2/v3/v4 clients attached to a v5 daemon must continue working. The daemon must omit unknown-to-peer messages and fields when serializing for older peers. Protocol v4 trace-event filtering remains in place from v0.12.2, and protocol v5 adds adapter-message filtering on top of it.
- A v5 client connecting to a v4 daemon must surface a clear version mismatch with operator-facing remediation (`upgrade the daemon to v0.13 to use adapter surfaces`) and must not synthesize adapter state locally.
- M0 must include proto-level handshake and envelope-decode tests for v2, v3, v4, and v5 envelopes against a v5 deserializer plus future-version rejection coverage. Daemon broadcast-filter tests land in M1 with the daemon wiring.

### Bootstrap and onboarding

Setup wizard adds a "Local personalization" step after the trace step. The step must:

1. Briefly explain what local personalization does (trains a small LoRA adapter from approved memory + accepted PERSONALITY.md, never sends data to a hosted provider) and how it differs from the v0.11 digest.
2. Ask whether to enable `learning.adapter_training` (default `no`).
3. If enabled: detect available real trainer backends on the operator's system (mlx-lm tooling on macOS Apple Silicon, llama.cpp tooling cross-platform), present the detected list, and ask the operator to allowlist one. Add the chosen backend identifier to `learning.adapter_training.allowed_backends` and the corresponding binary to `security.exec_allow`. The wizard names both keys explicitly. `FakeAdapterTrainer` is omitted from the normal wizard path unless the operator has explicitly enabled developer validation mode.
4. Ask whether to opt into trace-augmented training (`learning.adapter_training.capture_traces`, default `no`).
5. Show the compute cap default (7200s) and ask whether to override.
6. Print a summary line at the end naming `/settings show learning.adapter_training`, `allbert-cli adapters --help`, and `allbert-cli adapters training preview` for later inspection.

The setup state file (v0.12.1's `setup.state_path`) gains an `adapter_training` block so `allbert-cli setup --resume` resumes the personalization step correctly.

Existing-profile upgrade behavior:

- On daemon start, if the loaded `config.toml` has no `[learning.adapter_training]` section, the daemon attempts to insert the default block atomically with the v0.12.1 path-preserving TOML editor and emits an informational message to `daemon.log`: `Adapter training defaults installed (enabled=false). Use \`/settings show learning.adapter_training\` or \`allbert-cli adapters --help\` to inspect.`
- If safe insertion is not possible, the daemon skips the write, loads in-memory defaults for that boot, and emits a remediation hint.
- Existing profiles do not get a setup-wizard re-run.
- Default-write must be idempotent and skip if any partial `[learning.adapter_training]` block exists.

## Implementation design

### Rust dependencies and module layout

v0.13 adds no new top-level workspace crates. The implementation reuses crates already in the tree:

| Crate | What v0.13 uses it for |
| --- | --- |
| `serde_json` | Adapter manifest, active-adapter pointer, history.jsonl, eval JSON. |
| `sha2` | Corpus digest, adapter file digest, base-model digest. |
| `chrono` | Manifest timestamps. |
| `reqwest` | Ollama Modelfile registration via `POST /api/create`. |
| `tokio` | Trainer subprocess management, cancellation, progress channels. |
| `toml_edit` | Path-preserving `[learning.adapter_training]` default-write. |

Module layout:

- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/mod.rs` — module root. **Important**: `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapter.rs` already exists for `FrontendAdapter`. v0.13's storage module **must not** be named `adapter.rs`. Use the `adapters/` directory with submodules to avoid the collision.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/manifest.rs` — `AdapterManifest`, `BaseModelRef`, `Hyperparameters`, `EvalSummary` types and frontmatter codec.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/store.rs` — install/list/activate/deactivate/remove against `~/.allbert/adapters/`.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/trainer.rs` — `AdapterTrainer` trait, `TrainingPlan`, `TrainerProgress`, `CancellationToken`, error types.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/trainer_mlx.rs` — `MlxLoraTrainer`.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/trainer_llamacpp.rs` — `LlamaCppLoraTrainer`.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/trainer_fake.rs` — `FakeAdapterTrainer`.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/job.rs` — `PersonalityAdapterJob` implementing `LearningJob`.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/corpus.rs` — corpus assembly: durable + fact + episode summary + SOUL/PERSONALITY + opt-in trace excerpts; computes `corpus_digest`.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/eval.rs` — golden regression set runner, behavioral diff sampler, ASCII loss curve renderer.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/adapters/runtime.rs` — provider-side activation: per-session derived Modelfile registration for Ollama; passthrough/no-op for hosted providers.
- `crates/allbert-kernel/src/llm/ollama.rs` — extend with optional `active_adapter` plumbing.

Manifest ownership is split intentionally: `allbert-proto` owns the protocol DTOs used by daemon clients, while `adapters::manifest` owns on-disk manifest validation, schema-version checks, path handling, frontmatter/JSON codec, and conversions to/from the protocol DTOs.

The owned-trait choice deliberately avoids `candle`, `burn`, `tch`, `rig`, and any Python sidecar embedded in the kernel; ADR 0084 records the reasoning. Trainers are external executables under exec policy.

### `LearningJob` reuse

`PersonalityAdapterJob.run(ctx)` populates `LearningJobReport` per v0.11's contract. Existing `inputs`, `execution`, `resource_cost`, `output_artifacts`, and `staged_candidates` fields are populated as ADR 0084 specifies. `LearningJobContext` gains additive `compute_cap_remaining_seconds` and `cancel` fields; existing implementations do not consume them.

### Activity and trace integration

The kernel emits `ActivityPhase::Training` (a new variant) for the duration of a training run. Span emission piggybacks on v0.12.2's hook so a training run produces a `run_training` span with child spans for `corpus_assembly`, `trainer_invocation`, and `eval_run`. `gen_ai.operation.name = "train"` and `allbert.adapter.id` are the OTel-aligned attributes.

### Eval harness

Three fixed evals run on every training run:

1. **Golden regression set** (`~/.allbert/adapters/evals/golden.jsonl`). Operator-curated `(prompt, expected_style_hint)` pairs. Each pair is evaluated against base and adapter; the adapter must satisfy the expected style hint at the configured `min_golden_pass_rate`. v0.13 ships a 12-case bootstrap golden set covering core style/persona dimensions; operators add their own cases over time.
2. **Behavioral diff**. Sample N (default 24, configurable) recent turns from the corpus episode pool, replay the user prompt against base and adapter, render side-by-side outputs in `behavioral-diff.md` for review. No PII leaks: the rendering uses the same redaction posture as v0.12.2 traces.
3. **Loss curve**. ASCII loss curve generated from trainer-side step/loss data, plus a JSON sidecar for tools.

Eval output is part of the approval markdown's `eval_summary` block and is referenced by path; non-ML reviewers see one number (`golden_pass_rate`) and a markdown diff before deciding.

### Cross-cutting with v0.12.x

- Same emission boundaries: every training transition produces an `ActivityUpdate` and a span.
- Same redaction posture: ADR 0082's secret redactor runs again at corpus-build time when traces are opted in.
- Same settings hub: v0.12.1 typed registry plus path-preserving TOML edits.
- Same async loop and protocol seam: v0.12.2 bumped protocol to `4`; v0.13 bumps to `5` additively.
- Same self-change envelope: ADR 0080's review-first rule covers adapter approvals identically to patch approvals.

## End-user release plan

Release notes must explain:

- Allbert can now train a small local LoRA adapter from approved memory plus accepted `PERSONALITY.md`, gated behind explicit operator opt-in.
- Adapter training defaults to disabled. Operators opt in via setup wizard or `/settings set learning.adapter_training.enabled true`, allowlist a trainer backend, and add the trainer binary to `security.exec_allow`. Both gates are required.
- Training runs locally only and requires a compatible local base model plus trainer backend. Hosted providers ignore the active adapter and surface a one-line per-session notice.
- Activation is single-slot, base-model-pinned, and explicit. Accept installs but does not activate; activation is `adapters activate <id>`.
- A daily wall-clock compute cap (`learning.compute_cap_wall_seconds`, default 7200s) prevents runaway training.
- Adapter weights live at `~/.allbert/adapters/installed/<id>/` and are excluded from profile export by default. Corpus inputs (durable memory, accepted PERSONALITY.md, etc.) continue to travel; `--include-adapters` is the explicit opt-in for installed adapters plus `active.json`, not runs, incoming adapters, runtime caches, or history.
- Three fixed evals (golden regression set, behavioral diff, ASCII loss curve) accompany every adapter approval so non-ML reviewers can decide.
- The protocol bumps from `4` to `5`; v2/v3/v4 clients attached to v5 daemons keep working, and v5 clients connecting to older daemons surface a clear version-mismatch remediation.

Operator acceptance checklist:

- Fresh setup ends with the personalization step accepted, defaults written, and `/settings show learning.adapter_training` reflecting the choices.
- `allbert-cli adapters training preview` lists the corpus summary without invoking a trainer.
- A scheduled or on-demand `PersonalityAdapterJob` against the fake backend produces a run directory, an `adapter-approval` markdown, and a `LearningJobReport`.
- `inbox accept <aid>` records approval and installs; `adapters activate <adapter_id>` activates; `adapters deactivate` returns to base.
- Switching the active model auto-deactivates a base-model-mismatched adapter with a logged history entry.
- A turn against Ollama with an active adapter reaches the provider via the derived Modelfile.
- A turn against a hosted provider with an active adapter shows the one-line ignore notice once per session.
- Setting `learning.compute_cap_wall_seconds` low and starting a long run triggers cap refusal.
- `profile export` excludes `~/.allbert/adapters/` by default and lists the exclusion in dry-run output.
- `profile export --include-adapters` includes installed adapters plus `active.json`, excludes runs/incoming/runtime/history, and reports counts/bytes in the manifest.
- A simulated trainer crash leaves a `failed` manifest, not a partial install.
- Tier A validation passes with zero clippy warnings.
