v0.61 is the presentation-layer release of the pre-1.0 product arc: v0.60
designs the product experience (IA, navigation, screen composition, onboarding
flow, entry-point/CLI UX, First-Model Path, walking skeleton); v0.61 implements
that design as the polished primary 1.0 surface; v0.62 packages entry points;
v0.63 builds guided onboarding; and v0.64 is the integrated product RC. v0.61
turns the v0.58 design-system substrate — tokens, variant registry, pattern
library, one app shell, and the unified catalog — into a professionally-designed
product surface for the technical-prosumer 1.0 audience, filling in the v0.60
walking skeleton with real redesigned screens, without changing authority or the
catalog rendering boundary.

## Purpose

Implement the v0.60 information-architecture, navigation, and screen-composition
redesign — filling in the v0.60 walking skeleton with real, redesigned screens —
AND apply the brand, motion, visual-hierarchy, and dark-mode polish on that
redesigned surface, turning the substrate into the polished, professionally-
designed primary 1.0 product surface.

This closes both gaps the design work surfaced: the v0.60 Product Experience
Design found the app's information architecture and navigation model were
operator-utility-flat and needed a deliberate redesign (delivered as a model and
a walking skeleton, not as shipped screens); and the v0.58 review found motion
tokens and scattered transitions but no coherent product motion layer, an absent
brand identity, a thin `/` landing with no marketing surface, a utility-flat
hierarchy, and `system` theme behavior that needs explicit OS-preference
validation. v0.61 ships the redesigned IA and screens first, then brand, motion,
hierarchy, a real landing surface, suggested-action affordances, and validated
OS-driven dark mode — all on the existing token scales, all through the catalog.

## Operator-Visible Win

An operator moves through an app whose information architecture and navigation
were deliberately redesigned: a redesigned shell and navigation place workspace,
jobs, objectives, settings, and the operator panels where the v0.60 IA puts
them. They land on a real `/` marketing surface with the Allbert brand,
navigate into a shell carrying the wordmark instead of the stock framework logo,
see entrance/drawer/skeleton motion that respects their reduced-motion
preference, read redesigned screens with real visual hierarchy and populated
first-run/empty states that suggest what to do next, and get a dark theme that
honors their OS preference when `system` is selected — with no change to what the
app can do on their behalf.

## Information Architecture And Navigation Implementation (M1)

Implement the v0.60 information-architecture and navigation model on top of the
v0.60 walking skeleton. The redesigned app shell and navigation place the
primary surfaces (workspace, jobs, objectives) and the operator surfaces
(settings, operator panels) where the v0.60 IA puts them, with the navigation
structure, hierarchy, and screen slots the model defines. This is the structural
redesign the v0.60 Product Experience Design specified — now in scope and built
here, not deferred. It is expressed entirely through the catalog/shell/token
contract: the IA recomposes screens through the existing rendering substrate, it
does not add a new rendering path or new authority. The walking skeleton's
placeholder screens become the real navigation frame the redesigned screens fill.

## Redesigned Core Screens (M2)

Fill the redesigned shell with real screens. `/workspace`, `/jobs`,
`/objectives`, the settings surface, and the operator panel are recomposed per
the v0.60 information architecture — screen composition, slotting, and surface
placement follow the IA model, replacing the operator-utility-flat layouts.
Composition is done by extending the variant registry and pattern library (per
the v0.60 design-system gap analysis), not by hand-rolling per-page HEEx. Jobs
and Objectives stay folded into the catalog/shell (no regression of the v0.58
consolidation); the redesign recomposes them through the catalog, it does not
break them out. No backend, model, or data-layer change accompanies the
recomposition — the same DTOs render through redesigned screens.

## Brand Identity And Landing Surface (M3)

A real logo/wordmark replaces the stock Phoenix asset everywhere it appears
(redesigned shell header, mobile shellbar, favicon, app icons). The brand is
expressed as catalog/token-consuming assets and CSS, not as model- or data-
generated markup.

`/` becomes a real landing/marketing surface — composed per the v0.60 IA, still
rendered through the catalog inside (or as an accepted shape that emits) the
shell data contract, consuming tokens and the variant registry — with SEO and
Open Graph metadata (title, description, canonical, OG/Twitter card, OG image).
This replaces the ADR-accepted thin-landing exception noted in ADR 0074
consequences. No new route beyond this landing surface; `/workspace` stays
canonical.

## Motion Layer (M4)

An entrance/drawer/skeleton motion layer over the existing v0.58 motion-scale
tokens, applied to the redesigned shell and navigation. No new animation
primitives outside the token scale; durations and easings are token-driven.
Every transition is gated by the reduced-motion axis already in the token
system: with reduced-motion set, transitions collapse to instant. Motion is
presentation-only and introduces no new state or authority.

## Visual-Hierarchy Craft Pass (M5)

A depth/emphasis/density pass across the redesigned `/workspace`, `/jobs`,
`/objectives`, and operator panels, plus populated empty/first-run states —
executed on the redesigned screens from M1/M2, not on the old flat layouts.
Done by extending the variant registry and pattern library — elevation/surface
tones, emphasis variants, density options, and empty-state atoms — not by hand-
rolling per-page HEEx. Jobs and Objectives stay folded into the catalog/shell
(no regression of the v0.58 consolidation). This is craft layered on top of the
v0.60 information architecture: the redesign sets the structure, this pass sets
the depth, emphasis, and density within it.

## Dark Mode And Suggested-Action Affordances (M6)

OS dark-mode resolution: the `system` setting resolves app tokens to the OS
`prefers-color-scheme` preference instead of falling back to light, across all
redesigned pages, preserving the explicit light/dark overrides. Suggested-action
/ "what can I do" affordances appear on empty/first workspace views as view-only
entry points that compose registered-action DTOs through the catalog — shaped by
the v0.60 onboarding-flow design and the First-Model Path decision (ADR 0078),
so they seat the v0.63 guided-onboarding wizard along the designed onboarding
path rather than a blind guess. They grant no authority and do not implement the
wizard here.

## Security Posture

No new authority and no authority change. The catalog stays the rendering
boundary (ADR 0030/0074); the IA/navigation redesign, redesigned screens, brand,
motion, hierarchy, landing, and suggested-action affordances are all
presentation-only — the structural redesign recomposes screens through the
existing catalog/shell/token substrate and adds no new rendering path. Suggested
actions and landing CTAs render registered-action DTOs (ADR 0073) and grant no
authority or egress; launcher selection stays view-only. No model- or data-
generated markup is introduced. No internal rename (the v0.58 "Conversations"
UI-string convention is unchanged). No new route beyond the landing surface.
SEO/OG metadata is static and exposes no operator data or secrets.

## Non-Goals

- No model-generated UI; the catalog remains the rendering boundary.
- No new routes beyond the landing surface (the IA redesign recomposes existing
  surfaces; it does not add new routes).
- No new authority, capability, or egress; no authority change.
- No internal rename; "Conversations" stays UI-string-only.
- No rebuild of the rendering or data substrate: the v0.58 design-system
  substrate (tokens, variant registry, pattern library, catalog, shell) and the
  data substrate (canvas, tiles, ephemerals, Fragment/HMAC, persistence) are
  reused as-is. The structural redesign recomposes screens through them per the
  v0.60 IA; it does not rebuild them.
- No re-design here: the IA, navigation, screen composition, and onboarding flow
  are the v0.60 deliverables (ADR 0077/0078); v0.61 implements them, it does not
  re-decide them.
- No guided-onboarding wizard implementation (seated here per the v0.60
  onboarding-flow design, built in v0.63 after packaged entry points land in
  v0.62).
- No new animation primitives outside the v0.58 motion-scale tokens.
- No backend, model, or data-layer changes.
